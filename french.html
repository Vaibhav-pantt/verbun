Discover French History

Journey through the remarkable timeline of France — from ancient Gaul to the modern republic.

Ancient Gaul

Before Roman conquest, Gaul was inhabited by Celtic tribes. Julius Caesar's campaigns transformed it into a Roman province.

Carolingian Empire

Charlemagne united Western Europe and became the first Holy Roman Emperor in 800 AD, shaping medieval European identity.

French Revolution

The 1789 Revolution ended monarchy, sparked Enlightenment ideals, and introduced liberty, equality, and fraternity.

Napoleonic Era

Napoleon Bonaparte rose to power after the revolution, expanded French influence across Europe, and enacted legal reforms.

19th Century Upheavals

France cycled through monarchies, empires, and republics. Revolutions in 1830 and 1848 reshaped the nation’s political future.

Modern France

Post-WWII France saw the rise of the Fifth Republic under Charles de Gaulle and became a key EU and global power.
